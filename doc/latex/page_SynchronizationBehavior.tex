The On-\/\+Line Trajectory Generation (\mbox{\hyperlink{classOTG}{O\+TG}}) algorithms of the Reflexxes Motion Libraries are capable to generate


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{non-\/synchronized}},
\item {\bfseries{time-\/synchronized}}, and
\item {\bfseries{phase-\/synchronized}}
\end{DoxyItemize}

motion trajectories.

~\newline
~\newline
 

~\newline
 While the generation of non-\/synchronized and time-\/synchronized trajectories is always possible for both, the position-\/based and the velocity-\/based On-\/line Trajectory Generation algorithm, phase-\/synchronized trajectories require certain input values. The following image shows a non-\/, a time-\/, and phase-\/synchronized trajectory for three degrees of freedom.

~\newline
~\newline
 

~\newline
 To specify the behavior of the Reflexxes Motion Library, the enumeration \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5e}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+Sync\+Behavior\+Enum}} consists of four elements


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5ea1139782c92cf4c4cd0b13fc049661b00}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+N\+O\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+ON}},
\item \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5ea9f1e40a21dd62dbfb2413ef1d429547d}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+P\+H\+A\+S\+E\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+O\+N\+\_\+\+I\+F\+\_\+\+P\+O\+S\+S\+I\+B\+LE}},
\item \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5eac7ecf60f6d53a74ec2e1b51903b7907b}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+O\+N\+L\+Y\+\_\+\+T\+I\+M\+E\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+ON}}, and
\item \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5ea4386bfe9c6eea47eaeaa7956a06774e0}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+O\+N\+L\+Y\+\_\+\+P\+H\+A\+S\+E\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+ON}},
\end{DoxyItemize}

and the attribute \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_a86ba0c388c05fcee85dc57b2aefe5d21}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+Synchronization\+Behavior}} is used to specify the synchronization behavior of the desired trajectory.~\newline
~\newline
 Detailed information about the flag \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5ea9f1e40a21dd62dbfb2413ef1d429547d}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+P\+H\+A\+S\+E\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+O\+N\+\_\+\+I\+F\+\_\+\+P\+O\+S\+S\+I\+B\+LE}} can be found on page \mbox{\hyperlink{page_PSIfPossible}{About the Flag R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+P\+H\+A\+S\+E\+\_\+\+S\+Y\+N\+C\+H\+R\+O\+N\+I\+Z\+A\+T\+I\+O\+N\+\_\+\+I\+F\+\_\+\+P\+O\+S\+S\+I\+B\+LE}}.

\hypertarget{page_SynchronizationBehavior_sec_PositionBasedSyncronizazion}{}\doxysection{Synchronization Behavior of the Position-\/based On-\/line Trajectory Generation Algorithm}\label{page_SynchronizationBehavior_sec_PositionBasedSyncronizazion}
All four values of the set \mbox{\hyperlink{classRMLFlags_ac81441080dcaa610ff37c395ffc0bd5e}{R\+M\+L\+Flags\+::\+Sync\+Behavior\+Enum}} can be used to specify the synchronization behavior of the position-\/based On-\/\+Line Trajectory Generation algorithm (cf. \mbox{\hyperlink{classReflexxesAPI_a30d3cdba072553a5d53aa4ce4b4a77d1}{Reflexxes\+A\+P\+I\+::\+R\+M\+L\+Position()}}).~\newline
~\newline


An example showing, how to set-\/up time-\/ and phase-\/synchronized motion trajectories, is shown at page \mbox{\hyperlink{page_Code_03_RMLPositionSampleApplication}{Example 3 --- Different Synchronization Behaviors of the Position-\/based algorithm}}. The resulting time-\/ and phase-\/synchronized trajectories and paths of the two degrees of freedom of this example are depicted in the following figure.

~\newline
~\newline
 

~\newline
 

~\newline
 

~\newline
~\newline
 In this example, the task is to execute a motion from standstill, which is defined by $ {\bf W}_0\,=\,\left(\,\!_1\vec{W}_0,\,_2\vec{W}_0\right)^T $. After 1000 ms, a sensor event happens (unforeseen), and due to this, we have to immediately react and now have to reach a position ahead with a negative velocity. This desired state of motion, $ {\bf M}_{1000}^{\,trgt} $, is time-\/optimally reached after $ t\,=\,3873\,ms $ (cf. figure above), and right after this, a further switching of set-\/points happens, such that the originally desired state of motion, $ {\bf M}_0^{\,trgt} $, shall be reached again. After $ t\,=\,6756\,ms $, this desired state is finally reached (cf. \mbox{\hyperlink{page_Code_03_RMLPositionSampleApplication}{Example 3 --- Different Synchronization Behaviors of the Position-\/based algorithm}}).
